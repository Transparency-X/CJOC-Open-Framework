# Jurisdictional Boundaries and Constitutional Guardrails

To function effectively and survive legislative scrutiny (Pre-Legislative Scrutiny), the CJOC must have precisely defined boundaries. It must not infringe upon the constitutional independence of the Judiciary or the Director of Public Prosecutions (DPP).

## 1. In-Scope Entities (The "Criminal Justice Continuum")
The CJOC has jurisdiction over the **administrative, operational, and systemic integrity** functions of:
1.  **An Garda Síochána (AGS):** Systemic operational integrity, resource allocation, cross-agency cooperation, and internal affairs protocols.
2.  **The Irish Prison Service (IPS):** Conditions of custody, operational security, rehabilitation programs, and staff-inmate boundary management.
3.  **The Probation Service:** Community supervision standards, offender management protocols, and inter-agency risk assessments.
4.  **The Courts Service of Ireland:** Court administration, case flow management, IT infrastructure, and administrative staff conduct.
5.  **Forensic Science Ireland (FSI):** Chain of custody protocols, laboratory standards, and scientific integrity.
6.  **Office of the Director of Public Prosecutions (DPP):** Administrative efficiency, case file management systems, and victim communication protocols.

## 2. Strictly Out-of-Scope (Constitutional Boundaries)
Under *Bunreacht na hÉireann* (The Constitution of Ireland), the CJOC **shall not** have the power to:

### A. The Judiciary (Article 34 & 35)
*   **Boundary:** The CJOC cannot review, audit, or investigate the legal rulings, sentencing, or personal conduct of judges.
*   **Rationale:** Judicial independence is absolute. 
*   **Existing Body:** The **Judicial Council** (specifically the Judicial Conduct Committee) holds the exclusive statutory remit for judicial oversight.
*   **The Intersection:** If a systemic issue arises (e.g., massive trial delays), the CJOC may investigate the *Courts Service's* administrative scheduling or the *DPP's* file preparation times, but it cannot investigate the *Judge's* courtroom management.

### B. Prosecutorial Discretion (Article 30.3)
*   **Boundary:** The CJOC cannot direct the DPP to prosecute or drop a case, nor can it investigate the core decision-making process of *whether* to initiate prosecutions.
*   **Rationale:** The DPP's independence in prosecutorial decision-making is constitutionally protected.
*   **The Intersection:** The CJOC *can* audit the administrative systems the DPP uses to track files, the speed of evidence handover from AGS to the DPP, and the administrative compliance with the Victims of Crime Directive.

### C. The Legal Profession
*   **Boundary:** The CJOC does not regulate solicitors or barristers.
*   **Existing Body:** The **Legal Services Regulatory Authority (LSRA)**.

## 3. The "Intersection Rule"
When an integrity failure spans an In-Scope entity and an Out-of-Scope entity (e.g., a Garda leaking evidence to a private defense solicitor), the CJOC will investigate the **systemic and administrative failures of the In-Scope entity** (the Garda), while formally referring the Out-of-Scope entity (the solicitor) to the LSRA or the Garda Commissioner for disciplinary action.
