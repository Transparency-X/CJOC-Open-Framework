# Task 1.3.2: Targeted Consultation Sessions Plan

## Session Design Principles
-   **Chatham House Rule applies** to encourage candour from justice sector workers.
-   **Pre-circulated discussion prompts** (not open-ended Q&A) to maintain focus on the three consultation questions.
-   **Mixed formats** to accommodate different comfort levels with public speaking.
-   **Trauma-informed facilitation** for victim support sessions.

## Scheduled Sessions

### Session 1: Justice Sector Workers (Closed Door)
**Format:** In-person town hall (Dublin) + secure virtual option  
**Audience:** Garda representatives, prison officers, probation staff, Courts Service employees, FSI scientists  
**Focus:** Whistleblower safety, practical feasibility of audit powers, cultural barriers to integrity reporting  
**Facilitator:** Independent mediator (not DoJ official)  
**Key Discussion Prompts:**
1.  "If you discovered a cross-agency integrity failure today, what would stop you from reporting it?"
2.  "What would make you trust an external body like the CJOC over internal channels?"
3.  "Are there specific data points the CJOC should monitor that management currently ignores?"

### Session 2: Victim Support & Community Organisations
**Format:** Hybrid webinar with breakout rooms  
**Audience:** Victim Support Ireland, SAFE Ireland, ICCL, FLAC, Traveller justice groups, disability advocates  
**Focus:** Accessibility of CJOC complaints/referral pathways, victim communication failures, community trust metrics  
**Facilitator:** DoJ Victim Liaison Officer + external advocate co-facilitator  
**Key Discussion Prompts:**
1.  "Where does the current system fail victims at agency handover points?"
2.  "What would meaningful 'systemic integrity' look like from a victim’s perspective?"
3.  "How should the CJOC publish findings to maintain public trust without causing secondary harm?"

### Session 3: Academic Researchers & Data Experts
**Format:** Virtual workshop  
**Audience:** Criminologists, constitutional lawyers, data scientists, statisticians, GRECO/EU compliance experts  
**Focus:** Methodological rigour of data schemas, validity of proposed metrics, international comparators, research access protocols  
**Facilitator:** External academic chair  
**Key Discussion Prompts:**
1.  "Are the proposed data schemas sufficient for valid cross-agency analysis?"
2.  "What methodological flaws exist in the proposed thematic audit framework?"
3.  "How can the CJOC enable independent research while protecting sensitive data?"

### Session 4: Legal Profession & Oversight Bodies
**Format:** Roundtable (in-person)  
**Audience:** Law Society, Bar Council, Fiosrú, Inspector of Prisons, Ombudsman, SIPO, DPC  
**Focus:** Jurisdictional boundaries, referral SLAs, legal professional privilege, de-confliction protocols  
**Facilitator:** AGO representative  
**Key Discussion Prompts:**
1.  "Where are the ambiguity zones in Head 19 exclusions?"
2.  "What minimum SLA standards are needed for inter-body referrals?"
3.  "How should legal professional privilege be handled during systemic audits?"

## Accessibility & Inclusion Measures
-   Irish language interpretation available at all sessions.
-   Live captioning for virtual sessions.
-   Childcare stipend for in-person attendees.
-   Travel reimbursement for regional participants.
-   Written submission option for those unable to attend live sessions.

## Post-Session Protocol
-   Summary notes (anonymised) published within 5 working days.
-   Participants receive follow-up email with submission deadline reminder.
-   Dedicated contact point for post-session clarifications.
