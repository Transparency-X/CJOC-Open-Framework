# Task 2.1.2: Shadow Commission Board Establishment Protocol

## Purpose
Constitute a politically independent Shadow Board *before* full operational commencement to oversee CEO recruitment, approve founding governance documents, and advise on establishment priorities. The Shadow Board has advisory status only until the formal establishment day.

## Selection Process Design

### Cross-Party Oireachtas Selection Panel
| Member | Nominating Authority | Role |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Chair | Joint Committee on Justice (Government party) | Convene panel; cast deciding vote if tied |
| Member | Joint Committee on Justice (Opposition party 1) | Ensure cross-party legitimacy |
| Member | Joint Committee on Justice (Opposition party 2) | Ensure cross-party legitimacy |
| Member | Joint Committee on Justice (Independent/TG/SF) | Represent non-mainstream perspectives |
| Non-voting Advisor | Clerk of the Joint Committee | Procedural guidance; record-keeping |

### Eligibility Criteria (Statutory + Enhanced)
Candidates must meet statutory requirements under Head 8 AND demonstrate:
-   No current membership of any political party or elected office (5-year cooling-off period)
-   No current employment or contract with any in-scope agency (3-year cooling-off period)
-   No current registration as lobbyist under Regulation of Lobbying Act 2015
-   Demonstrated commitment to human rights, transparency, or public accountability through published work, professional practice, or civil society engagement

### Recruitment Timeline

| Phase | Duration | Key Deliverable |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Public advertisement + nomination window | 4 weeks | Minimum 15 qualified applicants per vacancy |
| Longlisting by independent HR consultant | 2 weeks | Shortlist of 8 candidates per vacancy |
| Panel interviews + written assessment | 2 weeks | Ranked list of 3 preferred candidates per vacancy |
| Panel recommendation to Minister | 1 week | Signed recommendation with scoring rationale |
| Government approval + presidential appointment | 2 weeks | Formal appointment letters issued |
| **Total** | **~11 weeks** | Shadow Board constituted |

## Shadow Board Mandate (Pre-Establishment Day)

The Shadow Board may ONLY undertake the following preparatory functions:
1.  Approve CEO job description and participate in selection panel
2.  Review and recommend Standing Orders, Code of Conduct, and Conflict of Interest policies for formal adoption post-establishment
3.  Advise DoJ on establishment budget prioritisation and interim staffing needs
4.  Receive briefings from existing oversight bodies on interoperability planning
5.  Approve public communications strategy for CJOC launch

The Shadow Board shall NOT:
-   Exercise any statutory powers under the CJOC Act
-   Enter contracts or incur expenditure (all resourced through DoJ)
-   Make public statements without DoJ clearance
-   Access live operational data from in-scope agencies
-   Hold formal decision-making meetings (advisory sessions only)

## Independence Safeguards
-   All panel deliberations confidential; individual scores not disclosed
-   Minister cannot reject panel recommendation without written justification laid before Oireachtas
-   Shadow Board members sign pre-appointment declaration of interests (enhanced version)
-   Media blackout on candidate identities until formal announcement
-   External probity advisor appointed to observe selection process

## Transition to Formal Board
On establishment day:
-   Shadow Board automatically becomes statutory Commission Board
-   Advisory mandate replaced by full statutory functions
-   Preliminary decisions (Standing Orders, etc.) formally ratified at first statutory meeting
-   Minutes of shadow sessions reclassified as official Commission records
