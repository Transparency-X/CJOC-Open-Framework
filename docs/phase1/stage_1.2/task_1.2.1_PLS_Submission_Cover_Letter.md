I am directed by the Minister for Justice to refer the enclosed General Scheme of the Criminal Justice Oversight Commission Bill 2026 to the Joint Committee on Justice for Pre-Legislative Scrutiny pursuant to Standing Order 178 of Dáil Éireann.

### Purpose of Referral
The proposed legislation establishes an independent statutory body to provide proactive, systemic oversight of integrity risks across Ireland’s criminal justice sector. This referral seeks the Committee’s expert scrutiny on three priority areas where parliamentary input is particularly valued:

1.  **Jurisdictional Boundaries:** Whether the exclusions in Head 19 adequately protect judicial independence and prosecutorial discretion while still enabling effective cross-agency oversight.
2.  **Interoperability with Existing Bodies:** Whether the proposed relationship with Fiosrú, the Inspector of Prisons, and the Ombudsman avoids duplication and ensures seamless referral pathways.
3.  **Investigative Powers Proportionality:** Whether the powers of entry and compulsion in Heads 21–24 are appropriately balanced against civil liberties and data protection rights.

### Enclosed Documentation
-   Approved General Scheme of the CJOC Bill 2026
-   Full Regulatory Impact Assessment
-   Data Protection Impact Assessment
-   Stakeholder Consultation Strategy Paper
-   Comparative Analysis of International Oversight Models
-   Summary of Attorney General’s Constitutional Advice

### Timeline Request
The Department respectfully requests that the Committee complete its scrutiny within **12 sitting weeks** of referral to align with the Government’s legislative programme and EU Anti-Corruption Directive transposition deadlines. The Department is available to provide oral briefings, written responses to queries, and facilitation of stakeholder hearings at the Committee’s convenience.
