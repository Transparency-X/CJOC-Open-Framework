## 1. WHY WE ARE CONSULTING
The Government has approved the General Scheme of the Criminal Justice Oversight Commission (CJOC) Bill. Before formal drafting begins, we seek your views on whether the proposed powers, structure, and scope will effectively deliver a more transparent, accountable, and resilient criminal justice system.

This consultation focuses on **three specific design questions** where public and stakeholder input can materially improve the legislation. We are not re-litigating the principle of establishing the CJOC; we are refining how it works.

## 2. THREE KEY QUESTIONS FOR YOUR INPUT

### Question 1: Are the investigative powers proportionate and sufficiently safeguarded?
The CJOC will have powers to enter premises without notice, compel documents, and require staff testimony (Heads 21–24). 
-   Are these powers necessary for effective systemic oversight?
-   What additional safeguards should be included to protect individual rights during systemic audits?
-   Should there be a judicial warrant requirement for certain categories of sensitive data?

### Question 2: Is the jurisdictional boundary correctly drawn?
The CJOC is explicitly excluded from reviewing judicial decisions or DPP prosecutorial discretion (Head 19).
-   Are there administrative functions of the Courts Service or DPP that *should* fall within CJOC oversight but are currently ambiguous?
-   How should the CJOC handle cases where systemic failure and individual misconduct are intertwined?
-   Are the referral mechanisms to Fiosrú, LSRA, and Judicial Council sufficiently clear?

### Question 3: How can whistleblower protections be strengthened beyond existing law?
The CJOC will operate under the Protected Disclosures Act 2014, but justice sector workers face unique retaliation risks.
-   What specific barriers prevent justice workers from reporting integrity concerns internally?
-   Should the CJOC have dedicated whistleblower liaison officers with specialised legal training?
-   What additional anonymity or anti-retaliation measures are needed beyond the 2014 Act?

## 3. SUMMARY OF PROPOSED MODEL
*(A 2-page plain-language summary of the General Scheme is attached as Annex A. Full General Scheme available at gov.ie/cjoc-scheme)*

| Element | Proposal | Rationale |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Independence** | Oireachtas-led appointments + separate Central Fund Vote | Prevents executive capture |
| **Scope** | 6 in-scope agencies (AGS, IPS, Probation, Courts Service admin, FSI, DPP admin) | Covers full criminal justice continuum |
| **Powers** | Entry, compulsion, binding Integrity Directives | Enables proactive systemic reform |
| **Exclusions** | No judicial/DPP decision review | Respects constitutional separation of powers |
| **Reporting** | Annual public report + EU/GRECO compliance data | Ensures transparency and international accountability |
