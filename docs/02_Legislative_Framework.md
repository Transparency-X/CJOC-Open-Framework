# Legislative Framework: Heads of the CJOC Bill

This document outlines the proposed structure (Heads) for the *Criminal Justice Oversight Commission Act*. This draft structure incorporates best practices from the *Electoral Reform Act 2022* (for structural independence) and the *Health Act 2007* (for investigative powers over large state agencies).

## Part 1: Preliminary and Establishment
*   **Head 1: Short Title and Commencement.**
*   **Head 2: Definitions.** (Defining "Systemic Integrity Risk", "Cross-Agency Incident", "In-Scope Agency").
*   **Head 3: Establishment of the Commission.** Establishes the CJOC as an independent statutory body corporate.

## Part 2: Governance and Independence
*   **Head 4: The Commission Board.** A 5-member board (Chair must be a legal professional; other members require expertise in data analytics, human rights, public audit, and public administration).
*   **Head 5: Appointment Process.** Board members appointed by the President on the advice of the Government, *strictly limited* to candidates shortlisted by a cross-party Oireachtas Committee (insulating from executive capture).
*   **Head 6: Statutory Independence.** Explicit clause stating the Commission shall be independent in the exercise of its functions and not subject to the direction of any Minister.
*   **Head 7: Independent Funding.** The CJOC shall have its own independent Vote in the national budget (Central Fund), rather than being subsumed under the Department of Justice allocation.

## Part 3: Functions and Powers
*   **Head 8: Core Functions.** To monitor, audit, and report on systemic integrity, operational efficiency, and human rights compliance across the criminal justice continuum.
*   **Head 9: Systemic Audits.** Power to conduct thematic reviews of policies, procedures, and resource allocation across multiple agencies simultaneously.
*   **Head 10: Inspection Powers (The "HIQA" Model).** Statutory rights for authorized CJOC officers to enter any premises of an in-scope agency without prior notice, compel the production of documents/data, and compel staff interviews.
*   **Head 11: Integrity Directives.** Power to issue binding directives requiring in-scope agencies to amend Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs) to mitigate identified systemic risks.

## Part 4: Cross-Agency Investigations
*   **Head 12: Jurisdiction over Intersections.** Power to investigate incidents where systemic failure or misconduct involves two or more in-scope agencies.
*   **Head 13: Mandatory Cooperation.** Imposes a statutory duty on all in-scope agencies to cooperate fully with CJOC investigations; creates an offense for willful obstruction or data withholding.

## Part 5: Accountability and Reporting
*   **Head 14: Annual Reporting.** Requirement to publish an annual "State of Criminal Justice Integrity" report and present it to the Oireachtas Joint Committee on Justice.
*   **Head 15: EU/GRECO Reporting.** Designates the CJOC as the national competent authority for compiling justice-sector data for EU Anti-Corruption and GRECO compliance reporting.
