# Alignment with EU and International Mandates

The CJOC framework is explicitly designed to close the compliance gaps identified by international monitoring bodies. This document maps the CJOC's functions to specific supranational requirements.

## 1. The EU Anti-Corruption Directive (Directive (EU) 2024/...)
*The EU mandates that member states establish comprehensive national anti-corruption policies and independent oversight/preventative bodies.*

| EU Directive Requirement | Current Irish Gap | How the CJOC Fulfills the Mandate |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Independent Oversight Bodies** | Oversight is fragmented across DoJ, DPENDR, and siloed inspectorates. | CJOC provides a single, independent statutory body dedicated to systemic integrity across the justice sector. |
| **National Risk Assessments** | No unified methodology for assessing corruption risks across the *entire* justice continuum. | CJOC's Data Analytics Pillar will publish annual, standardized systemic risk assessments. |
| **Coordination of Anti-Corruption Efforts** | Lack of a central coordinator for justice-sector integrity. | CJOC acts as the central node, formalizing MoUs and data-sharing between Fiosrú, SIPO, and the Ombudsman. |

## 2. Council of Europe GRECO (Group of States against Corruption)
*GRECO evaluates Ireland periodically. Past reports have criticized Ireland's integrity framework, particularly regarding the need for robust, proactive oversight mechanisms.*

| GRECO Evaluation Focus Area | CJOC Framework Response |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Police Integrity (AGS)** | While Fiosrú handles individual complaints, CJOC will audit systemic vulnerabilities (e.g., intelligence handling, off-duty conduct policies, cross-border data sharing). |
| **Judicial & Prosecutorial Integrity** | CJOC will strictly respect constitutional boundaries but will audit the *administrative* interfaces (e.g., Courts Service case management, DPP file-handling delays) where corruption or inefficiency can occur. |
| **Whistleblower Protection in Justice** | Aligns with DPENDR's *Protected Disclosures Act*, providing a secure, external, and specialized intake channel for justice sector workers who fear internal retaliation. |

## 3. Alignment with DPENDR (Whole-of-Government Frameworks)
To ensure the CJOC integrates seamlessly into the broader Irish public sector, its operational framework aligns with the Department of Public Expenditure, NDP Delivery and Reform (DPENDR):
*   **Freedom of Information (FOI):** The CJOC will operate under a presumption of maximum transparency, proactively publishing audit schemas and non-sensitive findings.
*   **Public Spending Code:** The CJOC will utilize its audit powers to ensure in-scope agencies are utilizing resources efficiently, reporting waste to the Comptroller and Auditor General (C&AG).
