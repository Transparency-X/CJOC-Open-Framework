# Task 1.1.2: Jurisdictional Boundaries – Draft Heads of the CJOC Bill

## Overview
This document defines the precise jurisdictional scope of the proposed Criminal Justice Oversight Commission. These boundaries have been drafted to withstand constitutional challenge and Pre-Legislative Scrutiny, explicitly respecting the separation of powers under *Bunreacht na hÉireann*.

---

## HEAD 2: INTERPRETATION – DEFINITION OF IN-SCOPE FUNCTIONS

### Proposed Text
> “In-scope functions” means, in relation to an agency specified in Schedule 1, those functions that are administrative, operational, procedural, or systemic in nature, including but not limited to case management systems, resource allocation, staff training protocols, inter-agency cooperation frameworks, IT infrastructure, compliance with statutory obligations, and adherence to human rights standards in service delivery.

### Explanatory Note
This definition deliberately limits the CJOC to *systemic and administrative* functions. It provides the legal hook for auditing how agencies operate without touching what they decide.

---

## HEAD 19: JURISDICTIONAL EXCLUSIONS

### Proposed Text
> (1) Notwithstanding any other provision of this Act, the Commission shall not have jurisdiction to—
>     (a) review, investigate, audit, or make findings concerning any judicial decision, ruling, sentencing determination, or case management order made by a judge acting in a judicial capacity;
>     (b) review, investigate, audit, or make findings concerning the decision of the Director of Public Prosecutions to initiate, continue, or discontinue a prosecution, or the content of any prosecutorial direction;
>     (c) regulate, investigate, or discipline any solicitor or barrister in respect of professional conduct;
>     (d) adjudicate upon any individual complaint of misconduct against a member of An Garda Síochána, prison officer, probation officer, or employee of an in-scope agency where such complaint falls within the statutory remit of another oversight body.
>
> (2) Where, in the course of exercising its functions, the Commission identifies evidence of matters falling within subsection (1), it shall—
>     (a) cease its inquiry into that specific matter;
>     (b) refer the evidence to the appropriate statutory body; and
>     (c) may continue its inquiry only insofar as it relates to systemic or administrative failures of an in-scope agency that may have contributed to or enabled the matter referred.

### Explanatory Note
-   **Subsection (1)(a):** Protects judicial independence under Articles 34 and 35 of the Constitution. The Judicial Council retains exclusive jurisdiction over judicial conduct.
-   **Subsection (1)(b):** Protects prosecutorial independence under Article 30.3. The DPP’s discretionary decisions are non-justiciable and non-reviewable by any statutory body.
-   **Subsection (1)(c):** Preserves the LSRA’s exclusive regulatory mandate over the legal profession.
-   **Subsection (1)(d):** Prevents duplication with Fiosrú, the Inspector of Prisons, and internal disciplinary processes. The CJOC is systemic, not individual.
-   **Subsection (2):** The “Intersection Rule.” Allows the CJOC to investigate the *systemic enablers* of individual misconduct (e.g., flawed evidence-handling SOPs that allowed corruption) while referring the individual actor to the appropriate body. This is critical for fulfilling the cross-cutting mandate without overstepping.

## SCHEDULE 1: IN-SCOPE AGENCIES AND SPECIFIED FUNCTIONS

| Agency | In-Scope Functions (Systemic/Administrative Only) | Explicitly Out-of-Scope |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **An Garda Síochána** | Operational SOPs; intelligence-sharing protocols; resource deployment; custody management systems; vetting procedures; cross-agency liaison frameworks; FOI compliance; victim liaison administration. | Individual misconduct complaints; operational policing decisions in specific incidents; national security intelligence operations (subject to separate statutory safeguards). |
| **Irish Prison Service** | Custody conditions; rehabilitation programme design/delivery; staff-inmate boundary protocols; healthcare administration; release preparation systems; inter-agency prisoner transfer protocols. | Individual prisoner complaints; parole/release decisions (Parole Board); security classifications of individual prisoners. |
| **Probation Service** | Community supervision standards; risk assessment methodologies; inter-agency information sharing; offender management protocols; programme evaluation frameworks. | Individual supervision decisions; pre-sentence report recommendations to courts. |
| **Courts Service** | Court administration; case flow management; IT systems; building management; administrative staff conduct; jury administration; accessibility services. | All judicial functions; courtroom management by judges; legal rulings. |
| **Forensic Science Ireland** | Laboratory standards; chain of custody protocols; turnaround time metrics; quality assurance systems; accreditation compliance; inter-agency evidence handover procedures. | Admissibility of forensic evidence in court; expert witness testimony. |
| **Office of the DPP** | Case file management systems; victim communication protocols; administrative efficiency metrics; inter-agency file transfer procedures; compliance with Victims Directive administrative obligations. | Prosecutorial discretion; charging decisions; trial strategy; legal advice to Gardaí. |

## CONSTITUTIONAL RISK ASSESSMENT SUMMARY

| Risk Area | Mitigation Strategy | Residual Risk Level |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Encroachment on Judicial Independence | Head 19(1)(a) + explicit reference to Judicial Council Act 2019 | LOW |
| Encroachment on DPP Discretion | Head 19(1)(b) + limiting audits to administrative/case management functions only | LOW |
| Duplication with Fiosrú | Head 19(1)(d) + mandatory referral protocol in MoU | LOW-MEDIUM (requires careful operational calibration) |
| Vagueness Challenge | Detailed Schedule 1 specifying functions per agency | LOW |
