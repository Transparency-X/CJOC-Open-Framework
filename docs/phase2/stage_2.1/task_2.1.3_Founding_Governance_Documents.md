# Task 2.1.3: Founding Governance Documents Framework

## Overview
Three foundational governance instruments must be drafted during the Shadow Board phase and formally adopted at the first statutory meeting. These documents operationalise the independence and integrity guarantees embedded in the CJOC Act.

---

## DOCUMENT 1: STANDING ORDERS

### Core Provisions
| Section | Content | Rationale |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Quorum & Voting | Quorum = 3 members including Chair; decisions by simple majority; Chair has casting vote | Ensures continuity while preventing minority capture |
| Meeting Frequency | Minimum monthly statutory meetings; extraordinary meetings on 48h notice by Chair or 2 members | Balances regularity with responsiveness |
| Agenda Setting | Chair sets agenda; any member may add items with 5-day notice; urgent items by majority consent | Prevents agenda control monopolisation |
| Minutes & Records | Draft minutes circulated within 5 working days; approved at next meeting; published within 10 days of approval (redacted as necessary) | Transparency default with operational flexibility |
| Delegations | CEO delegated authority for day-to-day administration up to €50K; above threshold requires Board resolution | Operational efficiency with financial accountability |
| Committees | Establishment, composition, and reporting requirements for Data Governance, Audit Standards, International Compliance committees | Embeds specialised oversight structures |
| Public Participation | Provision for open sessions on annual report presentation; closed sessions for investigations/personnel matters | Balances transparency with confidentiality needs |

---

## DOCUMENT 2: CODE OF CONDUCT

### Core Obligations
1.  **Impartiality:** Members and staff must perform functions without favour, prejudice, or anticipation of future employment with in-scope agencies.
2.  **Integrity:** Must uphold highest standards of honesty; refuse gifts/hospitality exceeding €50 value; declare all outside interests annually.
3.  **Confidentiality:** Lifetime obligation regarding unpublished investigative material; breach constitutes gross misconduct.
4.  **Respect:** Treat all persons with dignity; zero tolerance for harassment, discrimination, or bullying.
5.  **Accountability:** Cooperate fully with C&AG, Ombudsman, and DPC inquiries; respond to Oireachtas committee requests within statutory timeframes.
6.  **Collective Responsibility:** Support Board decisions publicly once made; dissent recorded in minutes but not expressed externally.

### Enforcement Mechanism
-   Breaches investigated by independent ethics officer (external appointment)
-   Minor breaches: written warning + training requirement
-   Serious breaches: recommendation to Minister for removal under Head 11
-   Criminal breaches: immediate referral to DPP + suspension pending outcome

## DOCUMENT 3: CONFLICT OF INTEREST POLICY

### Declaration Requirements
| Category | Declaration Timing | Scope |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Pre-appointment | Before Shadow Board service begins | All financial, professional, familial, and political interests (5-year lookback) |
| Annual renewal | Each January | Updated declarations + confirmation of no material change |
| Ad hoc | Before any meeting/agenda item | Any interest arising since last declaration relevant to business at hand |
| Post-service | For 2 years after departure | Notification of new employment/contracts with in-scope agencies or regulated entities |

### Management Protocols

| Conflict Type | Required Action | Record Keeping |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Actual conflict** (direct personal/financial interest in matter) | Recuse entirely from discussion, deliberation, and decision; leave room | Noted in minutes; declaration filed |
| **Potential conflict** (interest could reasonably be perceived to influence) | Declare at outset; Board decides whether recusal required | Decision + rationale minuted |
| **Apparent conflict** (no actual interest but reasonable perception exists) | Declare; Board may require recusal to preserve public confidence | Voluntary recusal encouraged; noted if declined |
| **Systemic conflict** (recurring pattern across multiple matters) | Board reviews role suitability; may recommend resignation or portfolio reallocation | Formal review documented; Minister notified if unresolved |

### Specific Justice Sector Prohibitions
Members and senior staff shall NOT:
-   Hold concurrent office in any political party or elected body
-   Provide paid consultancy to any in-scope agency or their contractors
-   Accept speaking fees from organisations with active CJOC investigations
-   Employ close relatives in positions reporting directly to them
-   Participate in audits/investigations involving former employers (3-year bar)

---

## Adoption & Publication Protocol
1.  Shadow Board reviews drafts with external governance expert
2.  Revised drafts circulated to DoJ for legal compliance check (not policy approval)
3.  Shadow Board recommends adoption subject to establishment day ratification
4.  First statutory Board meeting: formal resolution adopting all three documents
5.  Published on CJOC website within 48 hours of adoption
6.  Copies deposited in Oireachtas Library and sent to Joint Committee on Justice
7.  Annual review clause embedded in each document (first review at Month 12)
