# Executive Summary: The Criminal Justice Oversight Commission (CJOC)

## The Problem: The "Siloed" Oversight Gap
Ireland’s criminal justice system is currently monitored by a fragmented network of highly specialized, siloed oversight bodies. While agencies like **Fiosrú** (policing), the **Inspector of Prisons** (custody), and the **Judicial Council** (judicial conduct) perform vital functions, their mandates are strictly confined to their respective domains. 

This fragmentation creates severe **structural blind spots**. There is no unified, independent statutory body mandated to investigate cross-cutting integrity risks, systemic inefficiencies, or corruption networks that span *multiple* agencies (e.g., An Garda Síochána, the Irish Prison Service, and the Probation Service). Furthermore, the current model is highly **reactive**, waiting for individual complaints or public scandals to trigger investigations, rather than proactively monitoring systemic health.

## The Solution: The CJOC Model
The **Criminal Justice Oversight Commission (CJOC)** is a proposed statutory body designed to transition Ireland’s justice oversight from a reactive, complaint-driven model to a **proactive, systemic integrity model**. 

Operating under an "Oversight" framework, the CJOC will act as the connective tissue of the justice sector, focusing on the intersections where agencies meet. It will not replace existing bodies but will operate above them to monitor the health of the entire criminal justice continuum.

## Core Pillars of the CJOC
1. **Systemic Monitoring & Data Analytics:** Utilizing standardized data schemas to track cross-agency metrics, identify anomalies, and publish a "State of Criminal Justice Integrity" dashboard.
2. **Cross-Cutting Investigations:** Holding statutory powers to investigate integrity failures that span multiple agencies, bypassing jurisdictional paralysis.
3. **Proactive Auditing:** Conducting unannounced, thematic audits (utilizing "HIQA-style" inspection powers) to identify root causes of institutional failure before scandals break.
4. **International Compliance:** Acting as the centralized national monitoring body for the implementation of the **EU Anti-Corruption Directive** and **Council of Europe GRECO** recommendations within the justice sector.

## Purpose of this Repository
This open-source repository provides the conceptual, technical, and methodological framework to advocate for the CJOC. It contains draft legislative structures, data schemas, and policy mappings to empower researchers, journalists, and civil society to build the evidence base required for statutory reform.
