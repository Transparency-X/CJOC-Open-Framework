# CRIMINAL JUSTICE OVERSIGHT COMMISSION BILL 2026

## BILL
*Entitled*

**AN ACT TO ESTABLISH AN INDEPENDENT STATUTORY BODY KNOWN AS THE CRIMINAL JUSTICE OVERSIGHT COMMISSION; TO PROVIDE FOR THE SYSTEMIC MONITORING, AUDITING, AND INVESTIGATION OF INTEGRITY RISKS ACROSS THE CRIMINAL JUSTICE SECTOR; TO PROMOTE TRANSPARENCY, ACCOUNTABILITY, AND COMPLIANCE WITH INTERNATIONAL ANTI-CORRUPTION STANDARDS; AND TO PROVIDE FOR RELATED MATTERS.**

*BE IT ENACTED BY THE OIREACHTAS AS FOLLOWS:*

---

### PART 1: PRELIMINARY AND GENERAL

**Short title and commencement**
1. (1) This Act may be cited as the Criminal Justice Oversight Commission Act 2026.
    (2) This Act shall come into operation on such day or days as the Minister for Justice may appoint by order or orders either generally or with reference to any particular purpose or provision, and different days may be so appointed for different purposes or provisions.

**Interpretation**
2. In this Act—
    “Commission” means the Criminal Justice Oversight Commission established under section 4;
    “cross-agency integrity risk” means a systemic vulnerability, malpractice, corruption, or operational failure that involves, or arises at the intersection of, two or more in-scope agencies;
    “in-scope agency” means an agency specified in Schedule 1;
    “systemic integrity” means the structural, procedural, and cultural capacity of an in-scope agency to prevent corruption, ensure accountability, protect human rights, and operate efficiently;
    “Minister” means the Minister for Justice.

**Establishment day**
3. The Minister shall, by order, appoint a day to be the establishment day for the purposes of this Act.

---

### PART 2: ESTABLISHMENT AND INDEPENDENCE OF THE COMMISSION

**Establishment of the Criminal Justice Oversight Commission**
4. There stands established, on the establishment day, a body which shall be known as the Criminal Justice Oversight Commission (in this Act referred to as “the Commission”).

**Independence of the Commission**
5. Subject to this Act, the Commission shall be independent in the performance of its functions and shall not be subject to the direction, control, or supervision of any Minister of the Government, any Government department, or any in-scope agency in the exercise of its monitoring, auditing, investigative, or reporting functions.

**Seal of the Commission**
6. The Commission shall have a seal, and the seal shall be authenticated by the signature of the Chairperson or another member authorised by the Commission to act in that behalf.

**Principal functions of the Commission**
7. The principal functions of the Commission are to—
    (a) monitor and assess systemic integrity across all in-scope agencies;
    (b) conduct thematic audits of policies, procedures, resource allocation, and operational effectiveness within and across in-scope agencies;
    (c) investigate cross-agency integrity risks;
    (d) issue binding Integrity Directives to mitigate identified systemic vulnerabilities;
    (e) publish data, metrics, and reports on the state of criminal justice integrity;
    (f) serve as the national competent authority for compiling criminal justice sector data required under applicable European Union anti-corruption directives and Council of Europe GRECO evaluation frameworks;
    (g) promote interoperability and information sharing between existing oversight bodies in the justice sector.

---

### PART 3: MEMBERSHIP AND GOVERNANCE

**Membership of the Commission**
8. The Commission shall consist of 5 members, one of whom shall be appointed as Chairperson.

**Appointment of members**
9. (1) Members of the Commission shall be appointed by the President on the advice of the Government.
    (2) The Government shall only advise the President to appoint persons who have been recommended by a cross-party committee of the Oireachtas established for that purpose.
    (3) The membership shall collectively possess demonstrated expertise in—
        (a) law, constitutional governance, or public administration;
        (b) data science, digital systems, or forensic analytics;
        (c) human rights, criminology, or penology;
        (d) public finance, auditing, or regulatory compliance;
        (e) senior operational management within the public sector.

**Term of office**
10. A member shall hold office for a term of 5 years and shall be eligible for reappointment for one further consecutive term.

**Removal from office**
11. A member may be removed from office by the President on the advice of the Government only where resolutions have been passed by each House of the Oireachtas calling for the member’s removal on stated grounds of incapacity, misbehaviour, or conflict of interest.

**Chief Executive Officer**
12. (1) There shall be a Chief Executive Officer of the Commission.
    (2) The CEO shall be appointed through an open, competitive, and independent selection process administered by the Public Appointments Service.
    (3) The CEO shall be responsible for the day-to-day management, administration, and staffing of the Commission.

**Staff of the Commission**
13. The Commission may, with the consent of the Minister for Public Expenditure, NDP Delivery and Reform, appoint such and so many persons to be employees of the Commission as it considers necessary for the effective performance of its functions.

**Committees of the Commission**
14. The Commission may establish committees to assist it in the performance of its functions, including but not limited to a Data Governance Committee, an Audit Standards Committee, and an International Compliance Committee.

---

### PART 4: SYSTEMIC MONITORING AND AUDIT FUNCTIONS

**Systemic integrity monitoring**
15. The Commission shall continuously monitor the systemic integrity of all in-scope agencies through the analysis of administrative data, complaint trends, inspection reports from parallel oversight bodies, and other relevant indicators.

**Thematic audits**
16. (1) The Commission may, at its own initiative or upon request from the Oireachtas Joint Committee on Justice, conduct thematic audits of any in-scope agency or cross-agency process.
    (2) A thematic audit may examine policies, standard operating procedures, resource allocation, training programmes, IT systems, and cultural indicators relevant to systemic integrity.

**Data collection and analytics framework**
17. (1) Each in-scope agency shall provide the Commission with such data, records, and information as the Commission reasonably requires for the performance of its functions.
    (2) The Commission shall develop and maintain standardised data schemas to enable interoperable, cross-agency analysis while ensuring compliance with the Data Protection Act 2018.

**Publication of systemic findings**
18. The Commission shall publish the findings of its monitoring activities and thematic audits, subject to redaction only where necessary to protect national security, ongoing investigations, or personal data.

---

### PART 5: INVESTIGATIVE POWERS AND CROSS-AGENCY JURISDICTION

**Jurisdiction over cross-agency integrity risks**
19. The Commission has jurisdiction to investigate any cross-agency integrity risk involving two or more in-scope agencies, notwithstanding that individual elements of the risk may fall within the remit of another statutory oversight body.

**Power to enter premises**
20. An authorised officer of the Commission may, at any reasonable time and without prior notice, enter any premises occupied or used by an in-scope agency for the purpose of conducting an audit or investigation under this Act.

**Power to compel production of documents and data**
21. An authorised officer may require any person in an in-scope agency to produce any document, record, electronic data, or other material relevant to an audit or investigation, and may take copies, extracts, or possession of such material.

**Power to compel attendance and examination**
22. An authorised officer may require any employee, contractor, or former employee of an in-scope agency to attend before the Commission and answer questions relevant to an audit or investigation, subject to the protections afforded under section 34.

**Offence of obstruction or non-compliance**
23. A person who wilfully obstructs an authorised officer, fails to comply with a requirement under sections 20, 21, or 22, or provides false or misleading information commits an offence and is liable on summary conviction to a class A fine or imprisonment for a term not exceeding 12 months, or both.

**Referral of individual misconduct to relevant statutory bodies**
24. Where the Commission identifies evidence of individual misconduct during the course of a systemic audit or cross-agency investigation, it shall refer that evidence to the appropriate statutory body (including Fiosrú, the Judicial Council, the Legal Services Regulatory Authority, or the Director of Public Prosecutions) and shall not itself adjudicate on individual disciplinary or criminal liability.

---

### PART 6: INTEGRITY DIRECTIVES AND ENFORCEMENT

**Issuance of Integrity Directives**
25. (1) Where the Commission identifies a systemic integrity risk, it may issue an Integrity Directive to one or more in-scope agencies requiring them to amend policies, procedures, or operational practices within a specified timeframe.
    (2) An Integrity Directive shall specify the nature of the risk, the remedial action required, and the deadline for compliance.

**Compliance monitoring**
26. The Commission shall monitor compliance with Integrity Directives and may publish reports on non-compliance.

**Judicial review of Integrity Directives**
27. An in-scope agency may apply to the High Court for judicial review of an Integrity Directive on grounds of ultra vires, procedural unfairness, or unreasonableness within 28 days of issuance.

---

### PART 7: REPORTING AND ACCOUNTABILITY

**Annual Report on the State of Criminal Justice Integrity**
28. The Commission shall, not later than 6 months after the end of each financial year, prepare and publish an Annual Report on the State of Criminal Justice Integrity, which shall include systemic findings, audit summaries, data analytics, compliance assessments, and recommendations.

**Special reports**
29. The Commission may, at any time, prepare and publish special reports on urgent or significant matters arising from its functions.

**Accountability to the Oireachtas**
30. The Chairperson and CEO shall appear before the Oireachtas Joint Committee on Justice upon request to present and discuss the Commission’s annual report, special reports, and strategic priorities.

**International compliance reporting**
31. The Commission shall compile and submit criminal justice sector data and assessments required under applicable EU anti-corruption directives and GRECO evaluation frameworks, in coordination with the Department of Justice and the Department of Public Expenditure, NDP Delivery and Reform.

---

### PART 8: FINANCIAL PROVISIONS

**Grants to the Commission**
32. There shall be paid to the Commission in each financial year, out of moneys provided by the Oireachtas, a grant of such amount as may be sanctioned by the Minister for Public Expenditure, NDP Delivery and Reform, which grant shall constitute a separate Vote in the Central Fund distinct from the Vote for the Department of Justice.

**Accounts and audit**
33. The Commission shall keep proper accounts and shall submit them annually to the Comptroller and Auditor General for audit.

---

### PART 9: MISCELLANEOUS

**Protection of disclosures**
34. Nothing in this Act shall diminish the protections afforded to protected disclosures under the Protected Disclosures Act 2014, and the Commission shall maintain secure, confidential intake channels for whistleblowers within in-scope agencies.

**Confidentiality and data protection**
35. The Commission shall process all personal data in accordance with the Data Protection Act 2018 and the General Data Protection Regulation, and shall implement technical and organisational measures to ensure the security of sensitive information obtained under this Act.

**Regulations**
36. The Minister may make regulations for the purpose of giving effect to this Act.

**Amendment of other enactments**
37. The enactments specified in column (2) of Schedule 3 are amended to the extent specified in column (3).

## EXPLANATORY MEMORANDUM

This Bill establishes the Criminal Justice Oversight Commission (CJOC) as an independent statutory body tasked with proactive, systemic monitoring and cross-agency investigation of integrity risks within Ireland’s criminal justice sector.

The Bill addresses the structural gap identified by the Council of Europe’s GRECO evaluations and aligns Ireland’s domestic framework with the requirements of the EU Anti-Corruption Directive. It draws on the governance model of the Electoral Commission established under the Electoral Reform Act 2022 to ensure political independence through Oireachtas-led appointments and ring-fenced funding. Investigative powers are modelled on those granted to HIQA under the Health Act 2007 to ensure the Commission can effectively audit large, complex state agencies.

Critically, Part 5, Section 24 explicitly preserves the constitutional boundaries of the Judiciary and the Director of Public Prosecutions by limiting the Commission’s remit to systemic and administrative functions and mandating referral of individual misconduct to existing statutory bodies. The Commission is designed to complement, not replace, Fiosrú, the Inspector of Prisons, the Judicial Council, and the Office of the Ombudsman.
