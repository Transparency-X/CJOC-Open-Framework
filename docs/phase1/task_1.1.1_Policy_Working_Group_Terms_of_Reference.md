# Task 1.1.1: Cross-Departmental Policy Working Group – Terms of Reference

## 1. Purpose
To establish a dedicated, multi-disciplinary Policy Working Group (PWG) tasked with overseeing the drafting of the Heads of the Criminal Justice Oversight Commission Bill. The PWG will ensure the proposed legislation is constitutionally sound, operationally viable, and aligned with international best practice.

## 2. Composition and Membership
The PWG shall comprise representatives from the following entities, each with designated lead and alternate members:

| Entity | Role / Expertise Required | Representation Level |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Department of Justice (DoJ)** | Criminal justice policy, agency relations, legislative coordination | Assistant Secretary level (Chair) |
| **Attorney General’s Office (AGO)** | Constitutional law, legislative drafting, separation of powers | Advisory Counsel level |
| **Dept. of Public Expenditure, NDP Delivery & Reform (DPENDR)** | Public sector governance, FOI, whistleblower frameworks, budgetary architecture | Principal Officer level |
| **External Legal Academic (Constitutional Law)** | Independent constitutional scrutiny, comparative oversight models | Appointed by Minister on AGO advice |
| **External Legal Academic (Criminal Justice/Criminology)** | Systemic integrity, penology, international compliance standards | Appointed by Minister on DoJ advice |
| **Data Protection Commission (DPC)** | GDPR compliance, cross-agency data sharing, privacy-by-design | Senior Legal/Technical Advisor |
| **Office of the Parliamentary Counsel (OPC)** | Legislative drafting standards, statutory interpretation | Parliamentary Draftsman (Observer) |

## 3. Mandate and Deliverables
The PWG is mandated to deliver the following within **4 months** of establishment:
1.  Approved Terms of Reference for the CJOC (jurisdictional boundaries).
2.  Draft Heads of the CJOC Bill for submission to Government.
3.  Preliminary Regulatory Impact Assessment (RIA) screening document.
4.  Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) scoping report.
5.  Stakeholder consultation strategy paper.

## 4. Governance and Operations
-   **Meetings:** Fortnightly, with extraordinary meetings as required.
-   **Decision-Making:** Consensus-based. Where consensus cannot be reached, the Chair shall prepare a options paper for the Minister for Justice.
-   **Confidentiality:** All proceedings are strictly confidential under the Official Secrets Act. External academics shall sign NDAs prior to appointment.
-   **Secretariat:** Provided by the DoJ Criminal Justice Policy Division.
-   **Reporting:** Monthly progress reports to the Minister for Justice; final report to Cabinet Committee on Justice.

## 5. Conflict of Interest Protocol
All members must declare any current or past professional relationships with in-scope agencies (AGS, IPS, Probation Service, Courts Service, FSI, DPP). External academics with active research contracts involving these agencies must recuse themselves from relevant discussions or decline appointment.

## 6. Budget and Resources
-   External academic stipends: €15,000 per appointee (4-month engagement).
-   Secretariat costs: Absorbed within existing DoJ allocation.
-   Legal consultancy (if required): Subject to DPENDR sanction.
