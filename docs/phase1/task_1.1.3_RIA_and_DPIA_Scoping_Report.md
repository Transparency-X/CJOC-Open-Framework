# Task 1.1.3: Regulatory Impact Assessment (RIA) & Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) – Scoping Report

## PART A: REGULATORY IMPACT ASSESSMENT (RIA) SCREENING

### 1. RIA Screening Determination
**FULL RIA REQUIRED.** The CJOC Bill introduces significant new regulatory obligations on six major state agencies, creates a new statutory body with enforcement powers, and imposes compliance costs across the criminal justice sector. Under the Revised RIA Guidelines (DPENDR, 2023), a full RIA is mandatory.

### 2. Key Impact Areas to be Assessed

#### 2.1 Compliance Costs for In-Scope Agencies
-   **Data Provision Obligations:** Cost of adapting legacy IT systems to CJOC data schemas. Estimated initial capital cost per agency: €250,000–€750,000 depending on system maturity.
-   **Audit Response Burden:** Staff time required to respond to thematic audits and Integrity Directives. Estimated ongoing annual cost per agency: 1.5–3.0 FTE equivalents.
-   **Integrity Directive Implementation:** Cost of amending SOPs, retraining staff, and upgrading systems in response to binding directives. Highly variable; worst-case scenario for single directive: €1M+ (e.g., mandatory IT overhaul).

#### 2.2 Cost of Establishing and Operating the CJOC
-   **Year 1 Setup:** €3.2M (staff recruitment, IT infrastructure, premises, legal consultancy).
-   **Steady-State Annual Operating Cost:** €4.8M–€6.2M (based on comparable bodies: Electoral Commission €5.1M; HIQA €8.3M; Fiosrú €4.7M).
-   **Funding Source:** Separate Vote in Central Fund (not absorbed from DoJ or agency budgets).

#### 2.3 Benefits Assessment (Quantitative and Qualitative)
-   **Prevention of Systemic Failures:** Avoided cost of tribunals of inquiry (historical average: €20M–€50M per tribunal). Even preventing one tribunal every decade yields net positive ROI.
-   **EU Compliance Avoidance Costs:** Non-compliance with EU Anti-Corruption Directive could trigger infringement proceedings. Estimated potential penalty exposure: €5M–€15M.
-   **Operational Efficiency Gains:** Cross-agency data standardisation estimated to reduce duplicate reporting burdens by 15–20% across in-scope agencies.
-   **Public Trust Dividend:** Unquantifiable but significant; improved legitimacy of justice institutions.

#### 2.4 Competition and Market Impact
-   **Negligible.** The CJOC regulates state agencies only. No direct impact on private sector competition. Indirect benefit: more transparent procurement and contracting within justice agencies.

### 3. Consultation Requirements for Full RIA
-   Mandatory consultation with all six in-scope agencies.
-   Targeted consultation with DPENDR (Public Spending Code alignment).
-   Public consultation via gov.ie (minimum 6 weeks).
-   Engagement with Comptroller and Auditor General regarding value-for-money metrics.

## PART B: DATA PROTECTION IMPACT ASSESSMENT (DPIA) SCOPING

### 1. DPIA Trigger Assessment
**HIGH-RISK PROCESSING CONFIRMED.** The CJOC will process large volumes of personal data (including special category data relating to criminal convictions, health, and ethnicity) across multiple agencies for monitoring, auditing, and investigative purposes. A full DPIA is **mandatory** under Article 35 GDPR and Section 84 of the Data Protection Act 2018.

### 2. High-Risk Processing Operations Identified

| Processing Activity | Data Categories | Risk Level | Primary Risks |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Cross-agency data ingestion for systemic monitoring | Criminal conviction data; custody records; disciplinary records; demographic data | HIGH | Unauthorised access; function creep; re-identification of anonymised data |
| Investigative case files (cross-agency inquiries) | Special category data; witness statements; intelligence material | VERY HIGH | Breach confidentiality; prejudice to ongoing investigations; unlawful disclosure |
| Whistleblower intake channel | Identity of discloser; allegations; supporting evidence | VERY HIGH | Retaliation against discloser; unsecured transmission; inadequate access controls |
| Publication of audit findings | Aggregated/anonymised operational data; redacted case examples | MEDIUM | Re-identification through mosaic effect; reputational harm to identifiable individuals |

### 3. Legal Basis for Processing
-   **Article 6(1)(e) GDPR:** Processing necessary for performance of task carried out in public interest (statutory oversight function).
-   **Article 9(2)(g) GDPR + Section 54 DPA 2018:** Processing of special category data necessary for reasons of substantial public interest, with basis in law (CJOC Act), proportionate to aim pursued, and with suitable safeguards.
-   **Law Enforcement Directive (LED) Consideration:** Where CJOC processes data for law enforcement purposes (investigating criminal corruption), LED may apply alongside GDPR. Dual-basis processing framework required.

### 4. Required Safeguards (to be embedded in Bill and secondary legislation)
-   **Purpose Limitation:** Statutory prohibition on using CJOC-collected data for any purpose other than systemic oversight and cross-agency investigation.
-   **Data Minimisation:** Default position of aggregated/pseudonymised data for monitoring; identifiable data only for active investigations with documented justification.
-   **Access Controls:** Role-based access; mandatory security clearance for staff handling investigative files; audit logging of all data access.
-   **Retention Limits:** Statutory retention schedules (monitoring data: 3 years; investigation files: 7 years post-closure; whistleblower data: deletion upon resolution unless retained for evidential purposes).
-   **DPO Appointment:** Mandatory designation of a dedicated Data Protection Officer within the CJOC.
-   **Prior Consultation:** Formal prior consultation with the Data Protection Commission required before commencement of high-risk processing operations.

### 5. DPIA Timeline
-   **Draft DPIA:** Month 3 of Phase 1 (concurrent with Heads of Bill drafting).
-   **DPC Prior Consultation Submission:** Month 4 of Phase 1.
-   **Final DPIA:** Month 6 of Phase 1 (incorporating DPC feedback).
-   **Review Trigger:** DPIA must be reviewed upon enactment and annually thereafter, or whenever processing operations materially change.
