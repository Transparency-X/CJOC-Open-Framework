# Stakeholder Mapping and Interoperability

The CJOC will not operate in a vacuum. It must integrate seamlessly with existing oversight bodies through formal Memorandums of Understanding (MoUs) and statutory referral mechanisms. This document maps the CJOC's relationship with the broader Irish regulatory ecosystem.

## 1. Justice Sector Oversight Bodies

| Existing Body | Primary Mandate | Relationship & Boundary with CJOC |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Fiosrú** (Policing Oversight) | Investigates *individual* complaints against specific Gardaí. | **Complementary.** Fiosrú handles the "micro" (individual misconduct). CJOC handles the "macro" (systemic Garda vulnerabilities). Fiosrú must refer systemic patterns to the CJOC; CJOC refers individual misconduct discovered during audits back to Fiosrú. |
| **Inspector of Prisons** | Inspects prison conditions and custody deaths. | **Integrated.** The CJOC will work in deep integration with (or eventually absorb the systemic functions of) the Inspector of Prisons, expanding the inspection mandate to the entire justice continuum. |
| **Judicial Council** | Monitors judicial conduct and sentencing guidelines. | **Strict Separation.** The CJOC monitors the *Courts Service* (administration/staff). The Judicial Council monitors the *Judges*. A formal liaison protocol will ensure administrative reforms do not impinge on judicial independence. |

## 2. Whole-of-Government Integrity Bodies

| Existing Body | Primary Mandate | Relationship & Boundary with CJOC |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **SIPO** (Standards in Public Office) | Monitors integrity of elected officials and senior public office holders. | **Complementary.** SIPO monitors the *people* at the top of the political/administrative hierarchy. The CJOC monitors the *systems and processes* of the criminal justice apparatus. |
| **Office of the Ombudsman** | Investigates citizen complaints about administrative failures (maladministration). | **Referral Pathway.** The Ombudsman handles individual citizen grievances (e.g., "My FOI request was delayed"). The CJOC handles systemic integrity (e.g., "The FOI system is being weaponized to hide cross-agency corruption"). |
| **LSRA** (Legal Services Regulatory Authority) | Regulates solicitors and barristers. | **Referral Pathway.** If a CJOC systemic audit uncovers legal professional misconduct, the CJOC will refer the specific individuals to the LSRA. |

## 3. Data and Alignment Partners (DPENDR)

| Partner Entity | Role in CJOC Ecosystem |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Department of Public Expenditure, NDP Delivery and Reform (DPENDR)** | Ensures CJOC's data schemas, FOI protocols, and whistleblower intake systems align with whole-of-government standards (e.g., *Protected Disclosures Act 2022*). |
| **Central Statistics Office (CSO)** | Collaborates with the CJOC to ensure justice metrics published on the CJOC dashboard align with national statistical standards. |
| **Comptroller and Auditor General (C&AG)** | The CJOC will refer findings of systemic financial waste or misappropriation within justice agencies to the C&AG for formal financial audit. |

## 4. Handshake Protocols (MoUs)
To prevent "turf wars," the CJOC will draft statutory MoUs with the above bodies within its first 6 months of operation. These MoUs will define:
1.  **De-confliction:** Rules for determining lead investigator when a complaint touches multiple mandates.
2.  **Data Sharing:** Secure, GDPR-compliant APIs for sharing anonymized complaint data between Fiosrú, the Ombudsman, and the CJOC.
3.  **Referral SLAs:** Service Level Agreements for how quickly a body must acknowledge and act upon a systemic referral from the CJOC.
