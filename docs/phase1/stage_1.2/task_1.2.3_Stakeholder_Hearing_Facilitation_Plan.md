# Task 1.2.3: Stakeholder Hearing Facilitation Plan

## Overview
This plan outlines the Department’s facilitation of Oireachtas Joint Committee on Justice hearings during Pre-Legislative Scrutiny. Hearings are structured to elicit targeted, constructive feedback on the General Scheme rather than general policy debate.

## Proposed Hearing Schedule (4 Sessions)

### Session 1: Existing Oversight Bodies (Operational Interoperability)
**Focus:** De-confliction, referral pathways, data sharing, duplication risks

| Witness / Organisation | Role | Key Questions to Pose |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Fiosrú (Commissioner) | Individual Garda complaints | How should systemic patterns identified in individual complaints be referred to CJOC? What SLAs are needed? |
| Inspector of Prisons | Custody inspections | Where do current inspection gaps exist at AGS-IPS interface? What powers does CJOC need that you lack? |
| Office of the Ombudsman | Administrative maladministration | Which justice agencies currently fall outside your remit? How should CJOC-Ombudsman referrals work? |
| Judicial Council (Secretary) | Judicial oversight | What liaison protocol ensures CJOC administrative audits don’t impinge on judicial independence? |

### Session 2: Legal Profession & Prosecution (Constitutional Boundaries)
**Focus:** Jurisdictional exclusions, DPP interface, legal professional privilege

| Witness / Organisation | Role | Key Questions to Pose |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Law Society of Ireland | Solicitor regulation | Are LSRA-CJOC boundaries clear? What referral mechanism for solicitor misconduct discovered in CJOC audits? |
| Bar Council | Barrister regulation | Same as above + concerns about legal professional privilege during CJOC investigations |
| Office of the DPP (Legal Advisor) | Prosecutorial independence | Is Head 19(1)(b) exclusion sufficiently protective? What administrative data can CJOC safely access? |
| Constitutional Law Academic | Expert analysis | Are jurisdictional exclusions constitutionally robust? Any drafting vulnerabilities? |

### Session 3: Civil Liberties & Human Rights (Rights Safeguards)
**Focus:** Investigative powers proportionality, data protection, whistleblower safety

| Witness / Organisation | Role | Key Questions to Pose |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| ICCL | Civil liberties | Are Heads 21–24 proportionate? What additional safeguards needed for compelled testimony? |
| FLAC | Access to justice | Does CJOC improve access to justice for marginalised communities? Concerns about transparency? |
| Data Protection Commission | GDPR compliance | Is DPIA adequate? Concerns about cross-agency data ingestion or publication risks? |
| Transparency International Ireland | Anti-corruption advocacy | Does CJOC meet EU/GRECO standards? Gaps in preventive vs. reactive approach? |

### Session 4: Technical & Operational Experts (Implementation Feasibility)
**Focus:** Data infrastructure, staffing, international comparators

| Witness / Organisation | Role | Key Questions to Pose |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| CSO (Justice Statistics) | Data standards | Are proposed data schemas compatible with national statistical standards? |
| HIQA (Chief Inspector) | Systemic inspection model | Lessons from health sector oversight? What works/fails in large-state-agency inspections? |
| Electoral Commission (CEO) | New commission establishment | Practical lessons from standing up new independent body? Funding/budget realities? |
| DPENDR (Governance Division) | Public sector oversight | Alignment with whole-of-government integrity frameworks? Whistleblower protocol integration? |

## Facilitation Protocols

### Pre-Hearing Preparation
-   All witnesses receive General Scheme + Committee briefing paper **10 working days** in advance.
-   Witnesses submit written statements **5 working days** before hearing (published on committee website).
-   Department prepares Q&A brief for Committee members addressing anticipated criticisms.

### During Hearings
-   Department officials attend as technical advisors (non-speaking unless invited).
-   Real-time fact-checking support provided to Committee via clerk.
-   Sensitive evidence (national security, active investigations) taken in private session if requested.

### Post-Hearing Follow-Up
-   Department provides written responses to all unanswered questions within **10 working days**.
-   Consolidated submission addressing all stakeholder concerns delivered within **15 working days**.
-   Revised Heads of Bill (if amendments warranted) submitted within **20 working days**.

## Risk Mitigation

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Hearings become adversarial/politicised | Medium | Chair briefed to enforce focused questioning; witnesses pre-briefed on scope |
| Stakeholders raise issues outside CJOC remit | High | Department prepares jurisdictional boundary one-pager for distribution |
| Conflicting expert advice on constitutional points | Medium | AGO representative available for private session clarification |
| Media leaks of sensitive testimony | Low | Private sessions for sensitive evidence; embargo protocols for written submissions |
